## The situation

A Seattle-area fine-dining restaurant owner described Toast as comprehensive,
responsive, and meaningfully better suited to the restaurant’s needs than a niche
alternative. The restaurant recently switched from Aloha. This is valuable field
diligence: it makes the product moat more tangible.

It is not, by itself, proof of scalable owner economics.

## First-principles driver tree

```text
Net locations
× recurring gross profit per location
− hardware and implementation acquisition cost
− sales, support, R&D, and G&A
− cash taxes, capex, and working-capital needs
= pre-capital-return owner FCF
− cash required to offset SBC dilution
= dilution-adjusted owner FCF
÷ diluted shares
= owner FCF per share
```

## What would make the moat real?

- Additional restaurant-owner checks across independent, fine-dining, QSR, and
  multi-location operators confirm reliable implementation and support.
- Restaurants attach more modules and process more workflows without rising churn.
- Legacy systems are displaced because Toast improves operations, not merely because
  hardware is subsidized.
- Support quality remains strong as locations scale.

## Falsifiers

- Net additions slow while recurring gross profit per location fails to compensate.
- GPV per location declines structurally rather than cyclically.
- Support deteriorates, merchants resent payment pricing, or switching away accelerates.
- Buybacks consume cash but diluted shares continue rising materially.

## Questions you may have missed

<details>
  <summary>Open only after recording your decision</summary>

- Is Toast winning because its product is better, because switching is expensive, or because
  acquisition subsidies are attractive?
- Does the restaurant owner understand the full economic cost, including payment pricing?
- What happens to support quality when installed locations double?
- Are buybacks creating per-share value or merely paying employees indirectly?
- Which competitor is strongest for the exact restaurant segment Toast wants next?
</details>
