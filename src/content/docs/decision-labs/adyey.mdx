## The situation

Adyen’s consolidated story is easy to admire: enterprise payments, a unified
technology stack, global acquiring, and strong cash generation. The harder work is
to avoid modeling it as one smooth growth rate.

Digital, Unified Commerce, and Platforms have different customer acquisition paths,
competitive pressures, absolute growth contributions, and maturity curves. Value-added
services may deepen wallet share, but they must not be double-counted as a separate
economic engine without evidence.

## Build the company from its economic pillars

| Pillar | First-principles question | Key failure mode |
|---|---|---|
| Digital | Does Adyen keep and expand global enterprise wallet share? | Large merchants multi-source and compress pricing. |
| Unified Commerce | Does one commerce stack improve conversion and operating simplicity? | Omnichannel adoption is slower or less differentiated. |
| Platforms | Does embedded payments become durable, high-quality revenue? | Competition, implementation cost, or regulatory complexity rises. |
| Financial products and value-added tools | Do they deepen retention and economics? | Benefits are already captured in pillar growth and get double-counted. |

## Margin expansion audit

Do not assume a margin simply because the business is software-like. Derive it:

```text
Segment revenue growth and mix
− payment and network costs
− employee growth and compensation
− sales, implementation, compliance, and product investment
− depreciation, capex, taxes, and SBC
= owner FCF
```

Margin expansion is credible only when the underlying cost lines scale more slowly
than revenue for explainable reasons.

## Falsifiers

- Digital’s absolute annual revenue addition declines persistently.
- Unified Commerce or Platforms growth requires disproportionate hiring or implementation cost.
- EBITDA margin misses are explained repeatedly as temporary investment.
- Financial products add regulatory or capital risk without clear economic returns.
- Currency or ADR mechanics create a gap between business performance and per-ADR returns.
