## Positive expected value is necessary, not sufficient

A portfolio can contain attractive individual investments and still fail because:

- one position is large enough to cause ruin;
- several positions share the same hidden failure mechanism;
- probabilities and valuations are confidently wrong together;
- losses force selling before winners can mature;
- frequent trading interrupts long-duration compounding.

The portfolio objective is **geometric wealth**, not the average result of an isolated wager.
A 50% loss requires a 100% gain merely to recover. A 75% loss requires 300%.

<ChineseNote title="允许犯错，不允许出局">

## Why Buffett-style asymmetry differs from betting

The preferred source of asymmetry is a business with durable demand, pricing power, reinvestment
runway, and owner cash flow purchased below conservative value. Scenario math does not rescue a
weak business. It controls the consequences when sound underwriting still proves wrong.

<ExerciseReveal prompt="Five equally weighted positions produce four 40% losses and one 300% gain. Why is this not automatically a good strategy?">
Because the example assumes losses are capped, outcomes are sufficiently independent, the winner
is identifiable and held, and the probabilities are favorable. Real portfolios must prove those
conditions rather than admire the payoff diagram.
</ExerciseReveal>
