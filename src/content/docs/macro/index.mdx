## Why this is the backbone

A business can execute perfectly while its stock falls because the market changes the price
assigned to distant cash flows. A mediocre business can rally because financing becomes cheap
and investors accept lower prospective returns.

Treasury yields connect these outcomes. They are simultaneously:

- the market price for lending to the U.S. government;
- a baseline return available without underwriting a company;
- a building block for mortgages, corporate debt, currencies, and discount rates;
- a compact expression of expectations about inflation, real growth, policy, and risk.
