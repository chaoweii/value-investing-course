## Two channels must remain separate

### 1. Operating-business sensitivity

Rates can change actual revenue, costs, and cash flow:

- mortgage rates alter housing affordability and demand;
- refinancing rates alter interest expense and solvency;
- higher cash yields benefit businesses holding large investable balances;
- currencies alter translated revenue and competitive positioning;
- tight credit can slow customer expansion and increase churn.

### 2. Valuation-duration sensitivity

Even if the business forecast is unchanged, the present value changes:

```text
Present value = future cash flow / (1 + required return)^time
```

The farther away the cash flow, the more powerful the exponent. A one-point increase in the
required return can materially reprice a long-duration growth company without changing next
quarter’s revenue.

<ChineseNote title="双重冲击">
最危险的情况是经营与估值同时受压：高利率既降低客户需求，又提高折现率。最有韧性的情况则可能是业务现金流受益，同时估值压力可控。
</ChineseNote>

## The full transmission checklist

| Channel | Question for the investor |
|---|---|
| Discount rate | How much value sits in distant cash flows? |
| Debt and refinancing | When must liabilities be repriced, and at what spread? |
| Customer financing | Does the customer need affordable credit to buy? |
| Cash and float | Does higher short-rate income accrue to shareholders? |
| Currency | Does the rate differential move reported results or competitiveness? |
| Liquidity and multiples | Does the market demand a larger equity-risk premium? |
| Credit spreads | Is the risk-free rate falling while private financing still becomes more expensive? |
