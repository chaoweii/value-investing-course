## Voluntary opinions can become forced transactions

| Mechanism | Initial purpose | Stress transmission |
|---|---|---|
| Margin borrowing | Increase exposure | Falling prices trigger collateral calls and forced selling |
| Short selling | Express negative view or hedge | Rising prices can force buy-ins or short covering |
| Options | Transfer nonlinear risk | Dealers may hedge by buying into rallies or selling into declines |
| Securities lending | Enable shorts and settlement | Recall or scarcity can raise borrow cost and disrupt positioning |
| Circuit breakers | Slow disorderly trading | Pause execution; they do not determine fair value |

A market maker manages inventory and risk. It does not promise infinite liquidity. During stress,
quotes can widen or disappear because the cost of holding risk rises.

Settlement failures and collateral shortages matter because markets are networks of obligations.
The original thesis may remain unchanged while forced participants temporarily dominate price.
