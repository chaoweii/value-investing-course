## Passive funds cannot collectively own more than available float

If required exposure approaches available free float, the market does not produce extra shares by
wish. Instead, one or more adjustments occur:

1. prices rise until existing owners sell;
2. spreads and execution costs widen;
3. funds use futures, swaps, or other substitutes where permitted;
4. issuers or existing owners sell more shares;
5. trackers accept temporary tracking error.

## Does passive investing destroy price discovery?

Passive holders generally do not choose individual-company prices. Yet every quoted price is set
at the margin by shares that do trade. As passive ownership rises, a smaller active slice may
become more influential in determining price.

That can produce less price-sensitive mechanical demand around index events, but it does not make
valuation irrelevant. New information, issuance, active capital, arbitrage, and eventual cash-flow
delivery still discipline prices.
