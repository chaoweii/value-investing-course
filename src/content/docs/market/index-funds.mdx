## An index is a rulebook, not a pool of money

The index provider defines eligibility, constituents, float adjustments, weights, and effective
dates. It does not buy securities. Index mutual funds, ETFs, mandates, futures, and structured
products choose to track that rulebook.

An index addition can create buying even with no new investor inflow: existing trackers must move
from their old portfolio to the new weights. They may fund the addition by selling other holdings,
using cash, receiving new subscriptions, or temporarily using derivatives.

## Why trackers often trade near the close

Tracking error is measured against the index. If a change becomes effective at the closing price,
many funds prefer the closing auction because trading earlier creates a risk that their execution
price differs from the benchmark.

Fast entry, annual reconstitution, quarterly rebalancing, and corporate-action replacements are
different mechanisms. Never infer a forced deletion unless the methodology requires one.
