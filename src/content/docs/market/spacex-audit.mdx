## The index-mechanics audit

- Nasdaq-100 fast entry can evaluate a qualifying IPO after its seventh trading day and typically
  make the addition after 15 trading days. Eligibility still matters.
- Fast entry may temporarily increase the Nasdaq-100 above 100 issuers; it does not inherently
  require an immediate deletion.
- S&P 500 methodology generally requires an IPO to trade for at least 12 months before eligibility.
  Eligibility never guarantees committee selection.
- Existing trackers may need exposure even without new fund inflows. That demand can be funded by
  selling other holdings, using cash, or using derivatives.

## What the oversubscription does not prove

Orders above the offered amount are not a guaranteed pool of cash that must enter GOOGL, META,
AAPL, TSM, FUTU, or any thematic peer. Orders can be scaled, duplicated across managers, withdrawn,
financed, or redirected to Treasury bills and unrelated assets.

The disciplined claim is narrower: extraordinary demand is evidence of risk appetite and scarcity
for this offering. It may influence adjacent securities, but each transmission channel requires
separate evidence.
