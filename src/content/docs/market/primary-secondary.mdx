## The primary market funds the issuer

In an IPO or follow-on offering, newly issued or selling-shareholder securities are allocated
through underwriters. Only the issuer portion raises capital for the corporation.

```text
Investor cash → underwriter / offering process → company
Company → newly issued shares → investor
```

An oversubscribed order book means requested allocations exceed offered shares. It does **not**
prove that every order represents permanent, unlevered capital that must immediately buy something
else.

## The secondary market transfers existing claims

After listing, most trading is investor-to-investor:

```text
Buyer cash → seller
Seller's existing shares → buyer
Company receives no new capital
```

The company may still be affected through employee compensation, acquisition currency, reputation,
or future financing cost, but the ordinary trade itself does not fund operations.

| Event | Company receives cash? | Shares outstanding change? |
|---|---:|---:|
| Primary issuance | Usually yes | Usually increases |
| Selling shareholder in an IPO | No | No |
| Ordinary exchange trade | No | No |
| Company buyback | Company pays cash | Usually decreases after retirement |
