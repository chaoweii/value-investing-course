## Mutual-fund and ETF flows take different routes

A mutual-fund investor transacts with the fund at end-of-day NAV. Net subscriptions generally give
the manager cash to invest; redemptions require cash to be raised.

Most ETF trades occur on an exchange between investors. The ETF itself receives nothing when one
holder sells existing ETF shares to another.

## Authorized participants connect ETF shares to the basket

When ETF demand pushes the market price above NAV, an authorized participant may deliver the
underlying basket or cash, receive newly created ETF shares, and sell them. When ETF shares trade
below NAV, the reverse redemption process may remove ETF shares and release the basket.

This arbitrage mechanism usually keeps price near NAV. It can weaken during market stress, when
underlying securities are difficult to price or trade.
