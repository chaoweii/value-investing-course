## A quote is not guaranteed inventory

The best bid is the highest visible price a buyer offers. The best ask is the lowest visible price
a seller accepts. The spread is the distance between them. A market order prioritizes execution;
a limit order prioritizes price.

Every completed purchase has a seller. If no seller accepts the bid, no trade occurs. A motivated
buyer must raise the bid, wait, use a substitute, or remain unfilled.

## Why large orders move prices

Displayed supply is a schedule, not a single number. The first shares may be available at `$100`,
more at `$102`, and a much larger block only at `$110`. Consuming that ladder creates **price
impact**. Free float, ownership concentration, volatility, and urgency determine how steep it is.

Closing auctions concentrate many index and institutional orders at one clearing price. They can
handle enormous volume, but they still require offsetting interest.
