## Phase 0 · Decide before theory

Make an initial decision on TOST, ADYEY, and BRK.B. Each company exposes a different
failure mode:

| Company | The tempting shortcut | The harder owner question |
|---|---|---|
| TOST | “Owners love the product.” | Does that love become durable owner FCF per share at this price? |
| ADYEY | “The stock fell and the company is high quality.” | Does the downside remain controlled if growth and margins disappoint? |
| BRK.B | “It is safe, so it must be attractive.” | What return can its scale and capital allocation produce from today’s price? |

## Phase 1 · Think like a business owner

1. From trading symbols to owning businesses.
2. Evidence hierarchy and research audit trails.
3. Financial statements, owner earnings, SBC, buybacks, and owner cash.
4. Moats through demand durability, pricing power, switching costs, and expansion cost.
5. Management incentives and capital allocation.

## Phase 2 · Underwrite value and returns

1. Revenue-and-cost driver models.
2. ROIC, reinvestment runway, and margin expansion.
3. DCF, SOTP, and current-asset treatment.
4. Scenarios, IRR, margin of safety, and benchmark opportunity cost.
5. Falsifiers, conservative audits, sizing, and monitoring.

## Phase 3 · Build pattern recognition

Compare payments, networks, software, semiconductors, allocators, builders, consumer
brands, and experience-economy platforms. The goal is not to memorize ratios. It is
to learn which economic mechanism deserves which valuation method.

<ChineseNote title="路线图的核心">
真正的进步不是学会更多估值公式，而是知道在不同商业模式中，哪一个变量最容易让你的结论失真。
</ChineseNote>
