## Market event and mechanical-flow journal

Use `journals/market-event-journal.md` when an IPO, index change, redemption, short squeeze, or
other flow event appears to create an opportunity.

The journal requires you to separate:

- facts, estimates, and inference;
- forced participants from discretionary investors;
- physical-share demand from derivative exposure;
- temporary flow from long-term owner value;
- and an apparent opportunity from a valuation-supported decision.

Never let an event-flow explanation silently replace the underlying business thesis.

## Journal discipline

- Write before reading the full case.
- Date every decision and assumption change.
- Never delete an old conclusion; add a new entry explaining what changed.
- Separate new evidence from a change in market price.
- Record position-sizing logic, not only buy or sell opinions.
- Revisit falsifiers on a schedule and after material company events.

The browser progress tracker is convenient, but the Markdown journal is the durable
record of how your judgment evolves.

## Shareholder-ownership journal

Use `journals/shareholder-ownership-journal.md` before underwriting an unfamiliar security.
It records the share class, economic claim, voting rights, capital-stack priority, structural
limitations, and a management-integrity audit.

## Treasury-regime journal

Use `journals/treasury-regime-journal.md` to separate current market observations from macro
predictions. Complete it when a material yield move changes hurdle rates, business demand, or
portfolio sizing.

## Long-term portfolio policy

Use `journals/long-term-portfolio-policy.md` to preserve risk limits, sizing rationale,
valuation-band review triggers, and a dated record separating evidence-driven allocation changes
from price-only reviews.

## DCF underwriting journal

Use `journals/dcf-underwriting-journal.md` to preserve the normalized cash-flow anchor, assumption
evidence, owner-FCF bridge, terminal economics, reverse-DCF interpretation, and audit conclusion.
