## Margins are consequences

Forecast cost of revenue, sales and marketing, R&D, G&A, capex, working capital, and leases
independently. A cost line may scale more slowly than revenue only when a causal mechanism exists:
automation, fixed infrastructure, mature sales cohorts, better mix, or pricing.

Margin expansion becomes fragile when it requires:

- service quality to remain unchanged despite fewer resources;
- customer acquisition costs to fall without evidence;
- capex or working capital to disappear;
- every business segment to approach the best segment’s margin;
- reinvestment to fall while high growth continues.

## Toast checkpoint: identify the cost that actually scales

Toast's historical move from operating loss to operating profit is consistent with operating leverage,
but “scale” is not an explanation by itself. Underwrite each mechanism:

- **Hardware and implementation:** may be an upfront merchant-acquisition investment, but only if
  retention and later gross profit repay it.
- **Sales and marketing:** can fall relative to gross profit if mature cohorts expand and referrals help;
  it cannot fall merely because the spreadsheet needs a higher margin.
- **Support:** should not be squeezed until merchant loyalty breaks.
- **R&D:** a shared platform can serve more locations, while international and enterprise complexity
  may create new step-fixed costs.
- **G&A:** payments, lending, compliance, and public-company controls can scale, but do not disappear.
