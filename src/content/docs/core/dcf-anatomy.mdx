## First identify the claimant

```text
Cash flow available only to common shareholders
→ discount at required equity return
= intrinsic equity value directly
```

```text
Cash flow available to debt and equity providers
→ discount at WACC
= intrinsic enterprise value
→ bridge through debt, other claims, and excess cash
= intrinsic equity value
```

Accounting earnings measure economic activity under accounting rules. Reported FCF measures a
period’s cash movement. Owner FCF asks how much cash can belong to shareholders without weakening
the business. In this course, **equity owner FCF** means cash available to common shareholders
after operating needs, taxes, cash interest, required debt changes, capex, working capital, and
dilution offset. Its discounted value is equity value, not enterprise value.

## Equity owner FCF is the primary course method

```text
Revenue − operating costs
− taxes and cash interest
− capex, working capital, and required debt repayment
+ sustainable net borrowing
− cash required to offset dilution
= equity owner FCF
→ discount at required equity return
= operating equity value attributable to common shareholders
+ separately excluded owner cash, if applicable
= intrinsic common-equity value
÷ diluted shares
= intrinsic value per share
```

FCFF-to-EV remains a mandatory comparison method:

```text
FCFF available to debt and equity
→ discount at WACC
= enterprise value
− debt, preferred claims, and NCI
+ genuinely excess owner cash
= intrinsic common-equity value
÷ diluted shares
= intrinsic value per share
```

## Market cap is the market's offer, not a value to ignore

Market cap is essential after and around the intrinsic-value calculation:

- compare intrinsic equity value with market cap for margin of safety;
- compare intrinsic per-share value with the listed price;
- reverse the market cap to reveal implied assumptions;
- calculate market-implied multiples and opportunity cost;
- and size the position.

Market cap normally does not determine standalone intrinsic value. The important exception is
reverse DCF, where the purpose is to discover what the current market offer assumes.

## Five claim-matching warnings

1. Do not subtract debt twice.
2. Do not add owner cash twice.
3. Do not discount FCFF at the required equity return.
4. Do not match EV with net income or equity FCF.
5. Do not divide by basic shares when dilution is economically real.
