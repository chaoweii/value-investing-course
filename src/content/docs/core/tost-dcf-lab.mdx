This build-along uses the course's primary **equity owner-FCF** path. Discounted owner FCF produces
equity value directly. Toast is modeled as debt-light: the simplified learner model assumes no
material preferred claim, NCI, required debt repayment, or sustainable net borrowing. It adds only
separately underwritten owner cash, then divides by diluted shares.

## Source and assumption discipline

| Model item | Classification | Anchor |
|---|---|---|
| Historical revenue, operating income, cash flow, and capex | Reported fact | Toast filings imported through the read-only workbench |
| Q1 diluted shares and balance-sheet liquidity | Reported/derived fact | Q1 2026 release and owner-cash waterfall |
| Revenue drivers and mature cost ratios | Analyst assumption | Dependency-aware TOST operating model |
| SBC offset and valuation FCF | Derived owner-economics bridge | Reported cash flow, dilution, and repurchase mechanics |
| Discount rate and terminal growth | Investor judgment | Treasury opportunity cost, business risk, and mature economics |

The lab displays the dated snapshot beside every input. A newer price changes expected return; it does
not silently rewrite historical facts or business assumptions.

## Audit before concluding

1. Which assumption contributes most to value?
2. Is the Year 1 owner-FCF anchor normalized or merely annualized?
3. Does mature margin require weaker service or reinvestment?
4. Is SBC paid through dilution or repurchase cash?
5. Is owner cash counted only once?
6. Does the reverse DCF imply an achievable operating path?
7. Did positive restaurant diligence improve evidence confidence without directly changing a number?
