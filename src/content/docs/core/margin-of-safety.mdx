## Margin of safety is not a guaranteed future price

Long-term FCF, terminal value, discount rates, and future market recognition are all uncertain.
Margin of safety exists because you will be wrong about some of them.

The phrase does **not** mean:

```text
The stock must trade at my target price within a few years.
```

It means:

```text
I am paying a price low enough that imperfect forecasts, delayed recognition,
and some operating disappointment should not destroy the investment outcome.
```

<ChineseNote title="安全边际不是保证到价">
安全边际不是承诺市场几年内一定给你目标价，而是承认自己会错：现金流会错、终值会错、利率会错、市场认知时间也会错。价格要低到足以承受这些错误。
</ChineseNote>

## Three values, three different jobs

| Idea | Question | Common mistake |
|---|---|---|
| Intrinsic value today | What is the business worth now under conservative assumptions? | Treating one optimistic target price as fact. |
| Future value path | What could per-share owner value become if the thesis plays out? | Ignoring dilution, reinvestment, and time. |
| Future market price | What might other investors pay later? | Assuming recognition arrives exactly when your spreadsheet ends. |

A good investment should not require fast recognition of full value. Time can be your friend when
per-share owner value compounds, but it is your enemy when the only return comes from hoping
someone else soon pays a higher multiple.

## The practical test

Use scenarios as mechanisms, not decorations:

```text
Bear case: preserve capital or limit permanent loss
Base case: clear the benchmark after opportunity cost
Bull case: provide asymmetric upside
```

The bear case can be below today’s price. That does not automatically disqualify the investment.
But the downside must be sized, plausible, and survivable. If the base case cannot beat Treasury
bills, the S&P 500, BRK.B, or your next best idea, the investment is relying too heavily on the
bull case.

## Read the example

The default lab starts with:

```text
Current price: $70
Conservative value today: $80
Base value today: $110
Bull value today: $160

Bear outcome in 5 years: $75
Base outcome in 5 years: $135
Bull outcome in 5 years: $220
```

At `$70`, the investor is not relying on the bull case. If the conservative outcome only reaches
`$75`, the return is weak but capital is mostly preserved. The base case must still beat the
benchmark. The bull case creates asymmetry, but should not be the only reason to buy.

## What margin of safety protects against

- FCF grows more slowly than expected.
- Terminal value deserves a lower multiple or higher required return.
- Interest rates stay higher for longer.
- Recognition takes longer than your holding-period model assumes.
- Management dilutes shareholders or uses cash poorly.
- The business performs acceptably, but a better opportunity becomes available.

<ExerciseReveal prompt="Why is 'bear value above my purchase price' too strict as a universal rule?">
Because many attractive equities have real downside in severe scenarios. The right test is whether
the downside is controlled, the probability is acceptable, the base case beats alternatives, and
the position size keeps the portfolio alive if the bear case happens.
</ExerciseReveal>
