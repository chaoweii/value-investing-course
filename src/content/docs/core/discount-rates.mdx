## Two useful questions

**WACC asks:** what return does the market require from the company’s capital providers?

**Personal hurdle asks:** what return must this uncertain investment offer versus Treasury bills,
the S&P 500, BRK.B, and your best alternatives?

If “Treasury bills” or “cash yield” still feels abstract, review
[Fed Plumbing · Rates, Reserves, And The Balance Sheet](/macro/fed-plumbing/) first. That lesson
shows how Fed-administered short rates flow into money-market and T-bill opportunity costs.

Use WACC only to discount FCFF available to debt and equity providers. Use the required equity
return to discount equity owner FCF available only to common shareholders. The personal hurdle
also decides whether the resulting opportunity deserves your capital. Do not add an arbitrary
discount-rate penalty for every uncertainty already modeled in the cash-flow scenarios.

```text
Required equity return ≈ Treasury yield + equity-risk premium + company-specific risk judgment
```

Margin of safety is not a substitute for this required return. Use scenarios to model specific
business disappointments, and use the required return to express opportunity cost and broad equity
risk. If you both slash the cash flows and add a large unexplained discount-rate penalty, you may
be counting the same risk twice.
