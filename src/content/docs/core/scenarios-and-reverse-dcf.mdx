## Scenarios are different worlds

A bear case is not “base case minus 20%.” It should describe a coherent failure mechanism:
weaker demand, lower pricing, higher expansion cost, slower operating leverage, or dilution.
The bull case should require observable evidence before receiving a higher probability.

Sensitivity analysis ranks which assumptions carry the valuation. Reverse DCF asks what revenue,
margin, or cash-flow path makes today’s price mathematically reasonable. It does not prove that
the implied path will occur.

## Margin of safety is tested through scenarios

Do not treat margin of safety as one fixed discount percentage. Test it by asking:

```text
If the bear mechanism happens, how much permanent capital can I lose?
If the base case happens, does the stock beat my benchmark?
If recognition is delayed, does the IRR still justify the position?
If only the bull case works, why am I buying now?
```

The purpose is not to avoid every loss. It is to avoid needing perfect forecasts, fast
multiple expansion, or lucky timing for the investment to make sense.
