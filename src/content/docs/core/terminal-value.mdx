## Terminal value is the mature business, not the unexplained future

```text
Terminal value = FCF next year ÷ (discount rate − terminal growth)
```

The equation is easy. The economic consistency is difficult. A company cannot sustain high
terminal growth without reinvestment. Mature margins cannot remain exceptional without durable
demand, pricing power, and competitive defenses. Terminal growth must remain below the discount
rate.

Terminal value is an estimate of mature business value, not a guaranteed sale price. A future
market price may recognize less, more, or none of that value on your preferred schedule.

Audit the percentage of total value that comes from terminal value. A high percentage is not
automatically wrong, but it means the conclusion depends heavily on distant assumptions and
requires a larger margin of safety.
