## Company cash flow is not shareholder cash flow

```text
Operating cash generation
− maintenance and required growth capex
− structural working-capital needs and leases
= pre-capital-return owner FCF
− cash required to offset SBC dilution
= dilution-adjusted valuation FCF
```

Buybacks are capital allocation. Cash leaves first; shares decline only after repurchases exceed
SBC dilution. The same cash dollar cannot remain in net cash and also retire shares. Use current
diluted shares for current value; use a cash-funded share bridge for forward targets.

## Toast checkpoint: reported cash is not the final owner line

Toast can report positive FCF while issuing employee shares because SBC is added back as a noncash
expense in the cash-flow statement. That accounting treatment is correct, but the new shares are still
economic claims. The investor must either model dilution or reserve cash to offset it.

<SbcBuybackLab />

The honest comparison is not “share count before versus after the buyback.” It is:

```text
Shares without buyback after SBC
versus
shares after SBC and cash-funded buyback
```

A repurchase can reduce diluted shares and still be poor capital allocation if the company pays above
intrinsic value. It can also be sensible while merely preventing dilution, but then it should not be
described as free per-share growth.
