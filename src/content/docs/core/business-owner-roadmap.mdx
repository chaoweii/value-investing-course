## Purpose

Phase 1 replaces the habit of reacting to price narratives with a repeatable method
for understanding the underlying business.

| Lecture | Central question | Practical output |
|---|---|---|
| From trading to owning | What exactly do I own, and how does it create value? | Business-system map |
| Evidence hierarchy | Which claims are facts, calculations, assumptions, or judgments? | Evidence ledger |
| Owner earnings | What cash truly belongs to each diluted share? | Owner-FCF bridge |
| Moat mechanisms | Why can the business retain customers and economics? | Moat and falsifier map |
| Management and allocation | What happens to every retained dollar? | Capital-allocation audit |

## Expanded emphasis

This course will devote more time than the original tutorial series to:

- dynamic SBC and cash-funded buybacks;
- owner cash versus customer or merchant funds;
- demand durability and bargaining power;
- cost to enter new markets;
- management incentives and reinvestment discipline;
- inversion and thesis falsification.
