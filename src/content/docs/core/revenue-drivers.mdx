## Growth must have a physical explanation

For Toast:

```text
Locations × GPV per location × payments economics
+ locations × subscription attachment and price
+ adjacent fintech and services
= recurring economic revenue
```

## Toast checkpoint: do not let payment pass-through hide the engine

Toast's headline revenue includes substantial payments-related pass-through economics. For learning,
separate the drivers that create economic gross profit:

| Driver | What it tells you | What can go wrong |
|---|---|---|
| Ending locations | Distribution and installed-base growth | Low-quality additions churn or require subsidies |
| GPV per location | Merchant activity and customer mix | Restaurant weakness or movement toward smaller locations |
| Subscription gross profit | Module attachment and software monetization | Attach stalls or support costs rise |
| Fintech gross profit | Payment volume and retained economics | Pricing pressure or payment-cost inflation |
| Hardware/services gross profit | Cost of acquiring and enabling merchants | Losses remain structural rather than acquisition investment |

The next lesson uses these layers to test whether [product scale becomes owner value](/core/product-scale-to-owner-value/).

For Adyen:

```text
Merchant volume × net revenue yield
+ wallet-share expansion
+ Unified Commerce and Platforms adoption
+ financial-product attachment
= net revenue
```

TAM constrains what is possible. It does not prove market share, customer acquisition, retention,
pricing, or profitability. Always compare percentage growth with absolute additions: a declining
growth rate can still produce larger annual revenue additions.
